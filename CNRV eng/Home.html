Introduction

"Roman-Voda" National College is an preuniversitary educational institution from Roman, Romania, located in the center of the town. The building of the school is an historical monument, included in the "Neamt Country Historical Monuments" list. From a architectural point of view, it is identical with "Unirea" National College from Focsani and with "Ion C. Bratianu" National College from Pitesti.

The school has three administrative buildings: Building 'A' and building 'B'(a former high school for girls, reopened in 2016) which have classrooms for high school students and school labs, and building 'D' has classrooms for elementary school students and for middle school students. Also, Hogas House, today a museum, is part of the school.

SCHOOL'S MISSION

To provide quality services, matching the european standarts in the educational domain and to form responsible people, able to answer quick and efficient to the new requirements of the Romanian society.

SCHOOL'S VISION

To keep the school's status as a place of performance and european education, to emphasize the development of the values promoted through the school's philosophy - ""A school for who knows what it wants and for who wants to know!".

LOCATION

The school is located in the center of the town, near the Town Hall. Hogas House is also near the school, and it holds thousands of books and documents, written by personalities, that followed the classes of this school, or were teachers at this school.
